Wow, finally, another family like mine!! I was the eldest of seven and we
all loved vegetables. But our parents didn't send out those little
"uncertainty vibes" to give us any suspicions about them. =) I see parents
doing this all the time... Susie already has meat and potatoes on her plate
and they say, "Susie, would you like some...peas...sweetie?....?" The doubt
is there, behind their voices, and you better believe that every kid's radar
will pick it up! LOL (but sad)

One time I was at lunch with coworkers and we were chatting about this, and
one guy almost fainted when he heard that all the little kids in our family
had loved vegetables. He said that was the clincher--he had already thought
we were weird but now he was sure! Now that I've been around other families
a bit more, I do think it's funny that not only did ours love beans, corn,
peas and carrots; they thought broccoli, spinach, lima beans and Brussels
sprouts were fantastic! Brussels sprouts were introduced as Dad's favorite
vegetable, so of course they were going to be everyone's favorite...along
with Dad's favorite salad dressing (bleu cheese) and Dad's favorite (only)
soft drink (root beer).

Be prepared to be an idol, new dad Reinhard!
