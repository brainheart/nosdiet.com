....Hi Nancy :) Enjoy your weekend and don't lose hope :)
We all have our own special challenges and crutches we lean on, in
terms of food, and some of these crutches have been there for
literally years and years...
The first step in changing ourselves for the better, is simply to
acknowlege the existence of these obstacles. Now you know, without
doubt that you depend on sweets for comfort (and I'm trying to
remember whether your kids are all home with you, young, needing
lots of attention, etc...and if so who could blame you?) You need
energy and sugar is a very quick fix for that. Look even health
professionals I have known suffer from this sugar addiction. I
recall two surgeons I knew who were both sugar junkies... And they
*knew* sugar isn't good for the body in large amounts! So don't beat
yourself up, sugar is a formidable foe, especially if it is serving
as an emotional medication, as it is for so many of us.
So baby steps, Nancy :) Don't give up. I have been on NOS since the
last week of August and I was fairly serious in the first few weeks,
and then I became pretty unstructured from October till December.
Initially I lost around 6 lbs and took off 2 and a half inches from
my waist! I will be perfectly content to get through the Winter
here in NY and just not gain... Once Spring hits I will be walking
more and burning more calories, so I am sure I will lose more
then :) But as far as taking the rules really seriously I am re-
committed and ready to roll! Okay so this week you laid down some
important foundational groundwork and you know what your biggest
challenge is... it took you a long time to develop these habits, so
don't cave in and give up if it takes you a similarly long time to
break some of them... they are toughies.
If you can gradually reduce your sugar, as I have finally done with
my morning coffee (went from a whopping 4 teaspoons to two! Yikes,
and this took me a while before I was mentally ready to commit to
that, crazy as it may seem in retrospect) you will find that these
everyday adjustments will boost your own confidence and lead into a
happy cycle, rather than a vicious one... So give yourself a "tweak"
for being aware of your real diet "demons"... many people would
prefer to say, "I have bad genes" or "but I am hypoglycemic" or
other BS...You are looking reality in the face! Kudos! Could you
try to track the times when you are craving sweets? Also, what type
of sweets? Are they ice cream? Candy? Soda? If you recognize that
there's a real pattern going on, you will be more well equipped to
handle averting it ahead of time... Who knows, maybe you just feel
frazzled and drained and need some good vitamins? So much of our
daily dependency on food is mental. Like a baby with a pacifier, or
a security blanket... From a purely logistical and strategic point
of view I can say that if you don't bring it into the house to begin
with, you won't have to say "no" to it when the sugar crazies hit
you. And don't use your kids as an excuse to have loads of cookies
and soda around... I did that from Sept 2003 all the way through the
Winter and into Summer 2004, using my son as an excuse to buy crap!
We both gained a substantial amount of weight last year, me more,
but he too had quite a chubby tummy... Not healthy habits for sure.
Tough love was needed and now our sweets consumption is in the range
of 60-80 percent less! Neither of us feel deprived because we have
S days to enjoy ice cream. Usually we cheat once in a while but it
is so rare! Yay :)
Good luck Nancy... Please feel free to write as much as you need if
you are feeling weak.. I get alot of "comfort" just from my friends
here :) Applaud every little thing which you do for yourself to
shape your new destiny! :) (even if it means having 2 cookies
instead of 3, or 30! LOL :)
Be patient with yourself you are going to do great!
Peace and Love,
Deb
