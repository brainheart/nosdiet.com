Good to know there are some fellow no-S's this side of the pond, and beyond. I'm into my second week of it now and its going ok. I thought the no snacking would be easy as I rarely snacked before; but its surprisingly more difficult when you truly cannot cheat a little and have the odd packet of crisps or such, on a no-S day. I've also given up smoking in this last week (after 15 years of pretty much 20 ciggies every day, so this is no mean feat !!), and I'm starting a "three times a week" aerobics class from tomorrow . Eeekk!! Decided it was time for a complete life change, I thought - well - if yer gonna do it, might as well do it all at once!!!

I'm managing the non-smoking surprisingly well (only the odd severe pang), but I'm determined to stop; and with the No-S system in place it means I can't snack as a replacement, which is... well.... difficult... but much better in the long term. So - if I can get through the next weeks, I reckon It'll become a much easier daily system I get used to.

.... I hope...

Great to know of all the success stories; its very encouraging.

All the best for a happy, healthy and s-free new year
Eleanor
