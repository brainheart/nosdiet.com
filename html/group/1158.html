It's great that you were able to look at the scale
without really caring about it. I don't care, but I
tend to not even look, which is fine for me, but makes
it difficult for me to tell scale-watchers what to
expect. This is important, because I think that for
many people an even bigger problem than the direct
temptations of snacks, sweets, and seconds is managing
expectations; they expect a change of behavior to
immediately translate into measurable results and get
discouraged when it doesn't. They'd never have made it
to your first big drop. What they should keep in mind,
besides the fact that there will *be* plateaus, are
the considerations that you mention sustained you on
the plateaus: most importantly, the fact that the
consciousness of not being a glutton is a positive
pleasure in itself. The financial consideration is
also interesting, though I think I tend counterbalance
that by trading up on quality.
